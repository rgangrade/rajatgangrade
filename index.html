About Me


I'm a PhD candidate in Underground Construction and Tunneling at the Colorado School of Mines. I am being mentored by and work closely with Mike Mooney, Whitney Trainor-Guitton and Jacob Grasmick.

My research interests include visualization of tunneling and geotechnical data, characterization and analysis of spatial uncertainty, tunneling risk management, 2D and 3D subsurface modeling, optimization of site-investigation and monitoring programs and TBM-ground interaction.


My educational background includes a Masters' (Thesis) in Geotechnical Engineering from Virginia Tech. My research focused on Bayesian Analysis of liquefied soil cross-sections from Peru Earthquake (2007) to estimate the range of undrained shear strength of the liquefied soils. I was also a Teaching Assistant for Earth-Pressure and Foundation Engineering courses at Tech.


I completed Bachelors' degree in Civil & Environmental Engineering from V.J.T.I., Mumbai. During my undergraduate degree, I developed a strong technical skill-set from internship experience at Larsen and Toubro (L&T) and IIT-Kanpur

Characterizing Tunneling Risks

Geostatistical Modeling of Geotechnical Parameters

A visual and compelling characterization of the excavation environment and the tunneling risks is an essential task in planning, design and construction of tunneling projects. The uncertainty in the geological-geotechnical conditions is a significant source of tunneling risk.

The less information we know about the factors influencing a tunneling risk, more uncertainty exists into the risk incident, and less compelling measures are taken to mitigate the risk. A study on the impact of uncertainty on risk assessment and decision-making is being prioritized for tunneling and other engineering applications. The objective of risk assessment in a tunneling project is to provide reliable information for further decision-making to mitigate the risks.

Elements: Distribution of geotechnical parameters;

Characterizing the variability in the geotechnical parameters;

Modeling the spatial uncertainty in geotechnical parameters


Tools: PyVista , R, Python

Characterzing Transition Boundary

Geotechnical-Geological Transitions

I have developed a geostatistical framework to quantify the uncertainty in the location of the transition boundaries and evaluating mixed face conditions for soft-ground tunneling projects.

The results from the probabilistic assessment shall aid in selecting appropriate construction techniques and optimize tunneling performance. The uncertainty, expressed in terms of spatial locations and elevations, provides insights into the expected excavated volume of the soil materials and can be further applied to optimize soil conditioning regime, plan locations of TBM interventions and decide locations of additional geotechnical site-investigations.

Elements: Characterizing the proportion of soil units;

Quantifying uncertainty in longitudinal and transverse direction;

Evaluate mixed-face conditions for tunneling


Tools: PyVista , R, Python

Incorporating Spatial Uncertainty in Tunneling

Use of Uncertainty for Decision-Making and Risk Assessment

In my work, I have focussed on utilizing the knowledge of spatial uncertainty in the sub-surface domain of a tunneling project to identify locations of additional investigations and optimize site-investigation programs.

My work entails a comparative assessment of locating boreholes with the knowledge of spatial uncertainty in sub-surface domain versus the conventional approach of drilling boreholes in tunneling projects (using only a deterministic profile developed from prior investigations) and evaluates the reduction in spatial uncertainty

Applications: Optimization of site-investigation programs;

Assessment of construction means and methods and tunneling risks;

Improved tunneling performance


Tools: PyVista , R, Python
